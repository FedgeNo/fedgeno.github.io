What Is IDWT?

One wave. One integer. Two inputs. All fifteen particle masses, all forces, all quantum numbers — derived, not assumed.

One Wave on an Infinite-Dimensional Space

Infinite-Dimensional Wave Theory starts from a single postulate: reality is one complex wave Ψ∞ propagating on an infinite-dimensional manifold M∞. There is no separate electron field, no separate quark field, no separate photon field. There is one wave, and every particle is a bound resonance of it — the way a violin string has many harmonics but is still one string.

M∞ splits into two parts: ordinary time ℝt, and a hidden spatial manifold Ξ10. We live in the 3-dimensional observable universe, which in IDWT is Ψ∞ evaluated at a fixed position in the hidden directions — we are inside M∞, not projecting down onto a screen from outside.

The hidden space Ξ10 is not Planck-scale or compactified. It is macroscopic — its extent is set by the same coupling constants that determine particle masses. It is hidden because we occupy only 3 of its directions; particles with wider wavefunction support vibrate across directions we never move through, and we measure only the shadow of their activity in our 3D slice.

Six Sectors, Not Ten Random Dimensions

The hidden space does not have 10 equal directions. It decomposes into exactly six sectors, each a geometrically distinct manifold, with dimensions d ∈ {2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10}. The sector set is not a choice — it is uniquely forced by two mathematical requirements: (1) the sectors must form a Hopf fibration chain, and (2) the coupling coefficient must reach a precise critical value. Those two conditions together admit exactly six sectors and no others.

The sectors are nested: every direction present in sector d is still present in all higher sectors. The d=2 directions are inside d=3, inside d=4, all the way to d=10. This is not a hierarchy of separate spaces — it is one space progressively revealing more of itself to particles with the capacity to use more dimensions.

The Mass Formula

Every particle has a mode index n and lives in one sector d. Its mass is:

m(n, d) = S(n, d) × mscale,d

S(n, d) = C(n+d−1, d)  =  (n+d−1)! / (n! (d−1)!)

S(n, d) is a binomial coefficient — it counts the number of ways to distribute n energy quanta across d hidden directions. That is all it is. A particle with more hidden directions available, or a higher mode number, can distribute its quanta in more ways, giving it a higher multiplicity and therefore more mass.

Example: The electron has n=13, d=6. So S(13,6) = C(18,6) = 18564. The tau has n=4 (after correction), d=10. S(4,10) = C(13,10) = 286. The tau's mode index is actually n=76 — S(76,10) = C(85,10) ≈ 7.7×1012 — and its mass scale mscale,10 is correspondingly tiny, with the product landing at 1776.86 MeV.

The mass scales mscale,d are not free parameters — they are derived from one another by consistency conditions between sectors. The entire derivation runs from two inputs:

Input 1:   me = 0.511 MeV  (electron mass — the single dimensional anchor)

Input 2:   ns = 4  (the seed integer — an integer, not a continuous parameter)

Why ns=4? It is not a choice. It is the unique positive integer satisfying a fixed-point condition between sector d=4 and sector d=5. Two different cross-sector ratios are equal if and only if ns=4. No other value works.

From these two inputs, IDWT derives all six sector mass scales, all fifteen mode indices, and all fifteen Standard Model particle masses. The derivation is closed — no additional parameters enter.

Forces from Coordinate Containment

Every force in IDWT is associated with a sector. Electromagnetism lives in d=2. The weak force also lives in d=2, as a different mode excitation of the same Hopf fiber. Color (the strong force) lives in d=4, in the CP² geometry whose isometry group is SU(3)/U(2).

A particle couples to a force if and only if the force's sector coordinates are contained in the particle's sector. Since d=2 sits inside every higher sector (2 ⊂ 3 ⊂ 4 ⊂ 5 ⊂ 6 ⊂ 10), every particle has the photon's directions inside its wavefunction — which is why electric charge coupling is universal. Since d=4 is not inside d=2 or d=3, photons and down quarks do not couple to the strong force.

Neutrinos live in d=5. Their sector contains d=2 (giving weak coupling) but the d=5 Clifford algebra — specifically, the fact that 5 mod 8 = 5 — makes Majorana spinors geometrically impossible, not dynamically suppressed. Neutrinoless double beta decay is exactly forbidden, not merely rare.

Gravity has no sector. It is curvature of the full M∞, sourced by all mass equally. Every particle curves M∞ through the same mechanism regardless of which sector it occupies — which is why gravity is the one force that couples to everything without exception.

What Comes Out

From ns=4 and me=0.511 MeV alone:

  • All 15 particle masses — electron, muon, tau, three neutrinos, six quarks, photon, W, Z, Higgs — reproduced to better than 1% without exception, most to better than 0.1%.
  • The number of quark colors Nc=3 is derived, not assumed. It equals the Euler characteristic χ(CP²) = 3, the Atiyah-Singer index of the spinc Dirac operator on CP².
  • All three PMNS mixing angles from the same sector coupling constants; the CP-violating phase δCP = π + 2θ13 ≈ 197.1° (PDG: ~197°).
  • Newton's constant GN = G∞/V7, where V7 ≈ 113 is the product of sector localization lengths, fully derived from the same coupling constants.
  • Zero neutrinoless double beta decay — an exact geometric result, not a dynamical suppression.
  • No gravitons, no supersymmetry, no Kaluza-Klein towers, no WIMP dark matter — each forbidden by a specific geometric theorem, not excluded by parameter tuning.

Why This Is Different

The Standard Model takes quantum numbers as inputs: quarks have three colors because experiment says so; the electron is colour-neutral because it has never shown strong coupling; neutrinos are probably Dirac because no Majorana mass has been measured. The values are fitted; the structure is axiomatized.

In IDWT, these are not axioms — they are theorems. Nc=3 is forced by the Euler characteristic of CP². Colour-neutrality for the electron is forced by index cancellation on CP³. The Dirac condition for neutrinos is forced by Clifford algebra at d mod 8 = 5. The sector quantum number of a particle is not a label attached to it — it is the geometry of its sector expressing itself as a coupling structure.

One wave. One integer. Two real inputs. Everything else follows.
