The Coupling Web

There is one wave. Every link here is Ψ∞ coupling to itself, through the kernel, on the coordinates two of its sectors share — never an exchange between separate objects.

Edge strands — active structure
U(1) — electric charge
SU(2)_L — weak isospin
SU(3) — colour (quark block only)
Gravity couples every pair (it is omitted from the strands as universal).
Node size scales with the sector self-coupling g_dd; edge thickness with the rank-1 cross-coupling.

U(1) drops from any pair touching the neutral neutrino sector d=5; colour survives only in the quark block {d=3, d=4}; weak isospin is on every matter pair. The d=2 boson sector supplies the U(1) and SU(2)_L geometry rather than entering the matter pairings, with one exception: its Hopf-universal link g₂₅ to the neutrinos that fixes their mixing.
