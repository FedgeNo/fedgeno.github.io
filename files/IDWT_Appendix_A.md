**Anti-correlation with isometry dimension (T15f):** $g_{dd}$ decreases as the isometry group dimension increases. SO(4) (6 generators): $g_{33} \approx 21.2$; SU(3) (8 generators): $g_{44} \approx 4.5$; SU(4) (15 generators): $g_{66} = 1/4$ (at $d=6$). The physically strongest coupling sits in the sector with the smallest symmetry group. This is a trend across the three sectors named, not a law: the $d=5$ sector has isometry SO(6) with 15 generators — the same dimension as the $d=6$ SU(4) — yet $g_{55} \approx 0.133 < g_{66} = 1/4$, so equal isometry dimension does not fix equal coupling and the monotonic reading fails at $d=5$.

**T9a — both Hopf coupling products equal 96:**
- $g_{33} \times g_{44} = 8\sqrt{7} \times 12/\sqrt{7} = 96$ (the $\sqrt{7}$ cancels exactly)
- $g_{22} \times g_{55} = 722.5 \times (96/722.5) = 96$ (by construction of $g_{55}$)
- $96 = N_c(N_c+1)^3/2 = 3\cdot64/2$ (T15c)

The two independent derivation chains ($d=3\leftrightarrow d=4$ from vacuum fixed-point; $d=2\leftrightarrow d=5$ from Hopf universality) both yield 96 — this is the non-trivial content of T9a.

**Rank-1 coupling matrix:** $G_{dd'} = \sqrt{g_{dd}} \cdot \sqrt{g_{d'd'}} = v_d \cdot v_{d'}$. All cross-sector couplings factorise. The effective coupling is $g_{\rm eff}(n,d) = g_{dd} / S(n,d)$ (geometric dilution).

### §9. Key algebraic identities used in derivations

**Hockey-stick identity:**  
$S(n,d) = S(n,d-1) + S(n-1,d)$  
Used in every mode-index recursion step; the algebraic engine of the Generation Tower.

**Simplex ground state universality:**  
$S(1,d) = C(d,d) = 1$ for all $d \geq 0$.  
Forces $n_{\rm down} = 1$ as the unique universal ground state mode.

**Vandermonde combination rule for bosons:**  
$n_{\rm boson} = n_{\rm fermion} + d_{\rm partner} - 1$ (where $d_{\rm partner}$ is the mediating sector dimension).  
Source: Part 3 §11. Used to derive $n_W$, $n_Z$, $n_H$ from fermion quantities.

**Triangular number relation for $n_W$:**  
$S(n_e, 2) = C(14,2) = 91$; $n_W = 91 - n_{\nu_2} = 91 - 15 = 76$.  
The electron's $d=2$ simplex number partitions into W mode and second neutrino mode.

**Muon fixed-point self-consistency:**  
$n_\mu = S(n_s, 4) = 35$ AND $n_\mu = n_{\rm charm} + n_{\nu_2} = 20 + 15 = 35$.  
These being identical is the condition that forces $n_s = 4$.

**T4 closed-form ratio crossing (exact $n_s=4$ forcing):**  
$\dfrac{n(n+1)}{S(n,4)} = \dfrac{24}{(n+2)(n+3)}$ and $\dfrac{(n-1)\,n}{S(n-1,5)} = \dfrac{120}{(n+1)(n+2)(n+3)}$, since $S(n,4)=\binom{n+3}{4}$ and $S(n-1,5)=\binom{n+3}{5}$.  
Equating and cancelling $(n+2)(n+3)$ gives $24 = 120/(n+1)$, so $n+1=5$ and $n_s=4$ — unique over all reals, not merely the integers. Verified `idwt.py` STEP 25 (`t4_cross == [4]`). This supersedes the earlier phrasing of the crossing as a Gegenbauer weight $b_n(d)=\Gamma(d)\Gamma(n+1)/\Gamma(n+d)$ (corrected 2026-06-22): that Gamma form is inconsistent with the rational expressions above ($\Gamma(3)\Gamma(n+1)/\Gamma(n+3)=2/((n+1)(n+2))$, i.e. $1/15$ not $4/7$ at $n=4$). The rational identities and the $n_s=4$ conclusion are unaffected.

**Back-reaction denominator factorisation:**  
$n_{\rm up} \cdot n_s^2 \cdot S(n_s,4) = n_{\rm up} \cdot k_0 \cdot n_\mu = 3 \cdot 16 \cdot 35 = 1680$.  
Also $= n_s \cdot n_{\rm up} \cdot (n_s+n_{\rm up}) \cdot S(n_s,3) = 4\cdot3\cdot7\cdot20 = 1680$.

**$S^3$ Dirac cumulative-count bridge:**  
Per-sign degeneracy of the round-$S^3$ Dirac eigenvalue $\pm(k+\tfrac32)$ is $\deg_k=(k+1)(k+2)$; the cumulative per-sign count to level $k$ is $\sum_{j=0}^{k}(j+1)(j+2)=\tfrac13(k+1)(k+2)(k+3)=2\,S(k+1,3)$. Hence cum-to-$k{=}2 = 20 = S(4,3)$ and cum-to-$k{=}4 = 70 = 2\,S(5,3)$ — a tuning-free bridge from the $S^3$ Dirac tower to the simplex numbers. Verified sympy (`idwt.py` STEP 95).

**$S^3$ addition theorem (equipartition of a localized source):**  
On the homogeneous space $S^3=SU(2)$, group invariance and Schur's lemma make the per-level projector diagonal constant: $\operatorname{Tr}P_k(x,x)=\deg_k/\mathrm{Vol}(S^3)$ for all $x$ (scalar instance: $\sum_m|Y_{k,m}(x)|^2=(k+1)^2/(2\pi^2)$). A delta-localized (maximally point-like) source therefore carries norm $\deg_k/\mathrm{Vol}$ per level — *equal norm per state*. This is the identity behind the nucleon equi-norm step $P_L=C_0/C_K=2/70=1/S(5,3)$ (`idwt.py` STEP 95): equipartition across the tower is exactly maximal localization on $S^3$, not an independent assumption. Verified numerically (k=1 Cartesian-harmonic sum $=1$ to $4\times10^{-16}$).
