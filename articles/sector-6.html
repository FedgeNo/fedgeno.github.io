Sector 6 — Charged Leptons

CP³ geometry, total colour silence, and the sector whose Euler characteristic is the seed that determines everything else.

Geometry

The d=6 sector has geometry CP³ — the complex projective 3-space — with isometry group SU(4). CP³ is a Kähler manifold with six real dimensions. Its Euler characteristic \(\chi(\mathbb{CP}^3) = 4\), which in IDWT is the seed integer \(n_s = 4\). This connection — the lepton sector's topology directly setting the seed for the entire mass spectrum — means that all fifteen particle masses trace ultimately back to the geometry of CP³.

CP³ sits at the top of the complex Hopf chain up to d=6. The nesting \(\Xi_2 \subset \Xi_3 \subset \Xi_4 \subset \Xi_5 \subset \Xi_6\) means that the d=6 sector contains all five lower sectors inside it. An electron resonance carries coordinate support in all six of those nested sector directions — it is not confined to d=6 alone but contains the accumulated coordinates from d=2 upward, which is why it couples to electromagnetism and the weak force.

The electron is the single dimensional anchor of the entire theory — its mass \(m_e = 0.511\) MeV is the sole dimensional input from which all sector mass scales are derived. The electron's mode index \(n_e = N_c^2 + N_c + 1 = 13\) follows from the number of quark colours \(N_c = 3\).

The muon's mode index \(n_\mu = 35 = S(4,4)\) — the 4th simplex number in \(d=4\) — connecting the muon's position in the charged lepton tower to the quark sector indexing via Pascal's recursion. The mass ratio \(m_\mu/m_e = S(35,6)/S(13,6) = 3{,}838{,}380/18{,}564 \approx 206.8\) matches the observed ratio precisely.

The tau lepton, despite being a charged lepton, lives in the d=10 sector, not d=6. See Sector 10 — The Tau.

Total colour silence

The defining property of the d=6 sector as a coupling filter is complete, permanent, energy-independent absence of colour charge. This is not a suppression — it is geometric impossibility.

χ(CP³) = 4, not 3. The SU(4) isometry of CP³ contains SU(3) as a subgroup, but the four colour modes of CP³ do not transform in the SU(3) fundamental representation — the SU(4) structure places them in a representation where the SU(3) colour contributions cancel exactly. The CP³ geometry produces no surviving colour index. The electron has zero colour charge at every energy scale, not because it is a colour singlet that happens to be lighter than the confinement scale, but because the CP³ geometry makes colour-non-singlet states structurally unavailable.

The coupling filter for d=6: all QCD interactions are geometrically absent. The electron's electromagnetic coupling acts through coordinate containment — the d=2 photon coordinates are inside the d=6 electron sector (\(\Xi_2 \subset \Xi_6\)) — with coupling structure determined by the T2 rank-1 kernel and the SU(4) isometry restricted to its U(1) component.

The seed connection

\(\chi(\mathbb{CP}^3) = 4 = n_s\). This equality is not a coincidence — it is a theorem. The seed \(n_s\) must equal \(\chi(\mathbb{CP}^3)\) for the d=6 spectrum to be internally self-consistent: it counts the topological modes available in the lepton sector before gauge fixing removes one, leaving three lepton generations. Because \(n_s\) is the seed for all sector coupling constants and all mode indices, and because \(n_s = \chi(\mathbb{CP}^3)\), the entire mass spectrum of the Standard Model traces back to the Euler characteristic of the electron's sector.

Similarly, \(N_c = \chi(\mathbb{CP}^2) = 3 = n_u = n_s - 1\). The number of quark colours and the number of lepton generations are related by a single integer step, both determined by the geometry of their respective sector manifolds.

Coupling constant

The \(d=6\) sector self-coupling \(g_{66} = 1/n_s = 1/4 = 0.25\). This is a seed ratio — it equals the reciprocal of the Euler characteristic of the sector itself. It does not arise from a kernel fixed-point calculation (which is how \(g_{33}\) and \(g_{44}\) are derived) but directly from the topology of \(\mathbb{CP}^3\). The \(d=10\) sector shares this value: \(g_{10,10} = g_{66} = 1/4\), reflecting the \(\mu\)-\(\tau\) mass-scale symmetry between the two charged lepton sectors.
