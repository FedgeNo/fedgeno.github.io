The Infinite-Dimensional Wave Theory (IDWT) establishes a unified physical framework centered on the **master spinor field ($\Psi_\infty$)**. This field is the sole fundamental object, existing on an infinite-dimensional manifold from which all observable matter, forces, and quantum numbers emerge. The observer is located at $d=$3 — inside $\mathcal{M}_\infty$ as one of the occupied sector dimensions. The contents of reality are the modes of $\Psi_\infty$ that survive the framework's two-stage stability filter across the full sector structure.

Within this framework, particles are not independent entities but **geometric resonances** localized in macroscopic, non-compact hidden dimensions. The mass of each particle is determined by the **simplex formula $S(n,d)$**, which equates mass to the cumulative count of hidden microstates accessible at a specific mode level. These resonances are organized into **distinct sectors** ($d=$2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10) that correspond to the known families of the Standard Model. For instance, the $d=4$ ($\mathbb{CP}^2$) sector geometrically generates three colors of up-type quarks, while the $d=6$ ($\mathbb{CP}^3$) sector defines the charged leptons.

IDWT notably eliminates the need for **gravitons** by treating gravity as an emergent geometric curvature of the 3D slice caused by the concentration of the master field’s energy density. Because the hidden dimensions are not compact and contain no propagating gravitational degrees of freedom, the theory remains consistent with experimental null results for extra dimensions. Furthermore, the theory predicts that **neutrinos must be Dirac fermions**. This is a topological consequence of the $d=5$ sector geometry, which belongs to a Clifford algebra class that forbids Majorana mass terms, thereby predicting a zero rate for neutrinoless double beta decay.

The framework achieves high precision by deriving physical constants and particle properties from two integer seeds ($n_d=1$ and $n_u=3$), their composite $n_s=4$, and one unit of mass ($m_e$, the electron mass, used only to set the dimensional scale). These minimal inputs uniquely force the set of occupied mode indices and coupling constants, such as the **Cabibbo angle** and the **$W$, $Z$, and Higgs masses**. Ultimately, IDWT replaces the arbitrary free parameters of the Standard Model with **uniquely forced geometric invariants**, providing a rigid combinatorial structure where the generation of matter is a mathematical theorem of the underlying geometry.
