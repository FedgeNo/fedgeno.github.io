The Core Idea

IDWT is built from five postulates.

P1: reality is a single complex wave Ψ∞ on an infinite-dimensional manifold. P2: our observable 3D universe is a brane-like slice through this space. P3: the hidden dimensions are not compactified Planck-scale curls, they are macroscopic phase spaces. P4: what we call mass is inertia from coupling to hidden dimensions, proportional to the number of accessible microstates. P5: gravity emerges as the large-scale coherence of Ψ∞.

This gives a purely combinatorial mass rule: inertia scales with cumulative states on a d-dimensional hidden sphere.

m = m_scale,d × S(n,d)   where   S(n,d) = C(n+d-1, d)

Why It Works

  • a
    Sector structure from geometry. Hopf fibrations select the allowed hidden dimensions: d = 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10. Each d defines a particle sector.
  • b
    Generation law is a theorem. The hockey-stick identity for binomial sums gives S(n+1,d) = S(n,d) + C(n+d, d-1), so mass ratios across generations follow exactly.
  • c
    One input, twelve outputs. Using only the electron mass me as scale, IDWT predicts 12 lepton, quark, and boson masses to better than 1 percent.
