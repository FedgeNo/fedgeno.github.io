### §3. The sector set D = {2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10}

**What it is.** Six geometric strata of Ξ₁₀, each hosting one particle family. Documented in Part 1 §3 and the Euler characteristic chain article.

**The six dimensions as CP^k (even-d sectors) and S^k (odd-d sectors):**
- d=2: CP¹ (isometry U(1))
- d=3: S³ (isometry SO(4))
- d=4: CP² (isometry SU(3))
- d=5: S⁵ (isometry SO(6) ≅ SU(4))
- d=6: CP³ (isometry SU(4))
- d=10: CP⁵ (isometry SU(6))

**Why d=7, 8, 9 are absent.** The coupling fixed-point equation for g₈₈ has no solution (d=8 gap). The terminal sector is set by Gegenbauer criticality: b_{k₀}(d) = ½ exactly at d = 2(N_c+2) = 10 (Theorem T5, Part 1 §4b). The sequence is consecutive from d=2 through d=6, then jumps to d=10.

**Jump from d=6 to d=10:**  d=10 − d=6 = 4 = n_s. The final sector is n_s steps above the penultimate sector in dimension.

**Sum of occupied dimensions:** 2+3+4+5+6+10 = 30 = (n_s+1) × N_f = 5 × 6. (Not assigned structural meaning yet; noted for reference.)

**Products within the set:** 2×3 = 6 ∈ D, 2×5 = 10 ∈ D. Products 3×4=12 and 2×6=12 are not in D. The two products that land in D both involve d=2.

### §9. Key algebraic identities used in derivations

**Hockey-stick identity:**  
S(n,d) = S(n,d−1) + S(n−1,d)  
Used in every mode-index recursion step; the algebraic engine of the Generation Tower.

**Simplex ground state universality:**  
S(1,d) = C(d,d) = 1 for all d ≥ 0.  
Forces n_down = 1 as the unique universal ground state mode.

**Vandermonde combination rule for bosons:**  
n_boson = n_fermion + d_partner − 1 (where d_partner is the mediating sector dimension).  
Source: Part 3 §11. Used to derive n_W, n_Z, n_H from fermion quantities.

**Triangular number relation for n_W:**  
S(n_e, 2) = C(14,2) = 91; n_W = 91 − n_ν₂ = 91 − 15 = 76.  
The electron's d=2 simplex number partitions into W mode and second neutrino mode.

**Muon fixed-point self-consistency:**  
n_μ = S(n_s, 4) = 35 AND n_μ = n_charm + n_ν₂ = 20 + 15 = 35.  
These being identical is the condition that forces n_s = 4.

**Back-reaction denominator factorisation:**

### §10. New additive forms of the mode indices

**Third form of n_top:**  
n_μ + n_ν₂ + n_ν₃ = 35 + 15 + 22 = **72 = n_top**  
The entire light lepton index set (muon plus both heavier neutrinos) sums to the top quark mode index. No S(n,d) is involved. The two documented forms are the Euler-characteristic product N_c·n_s·N_f and the triangular-number relation S(n_e,2)−n_charm+1; this is a third independent characterisation.

**Higgs as top plus tau:**  
n_top + n_τ = 72 + 23 = **95 = n_H**  
Equivalent to the documented n_up + n_charm + n_top = 95 via n_τ = n_up + n_charm, but the two-term form is cleaner.

**Muon as product of two sector-adjacent integers:**  
(n_s + 1)(n_s + n_up) = 5 · 7 = **35 = n_μ**  
The factor 7 = n_s + n_up is the same quantity under the square root in g₃₃ = n_s² √(n_s+n_up)/2. The muon mode index is a product of consecutive integers around the coupling geometry parameter.

**Electron plus tau equals N_f squared:**  
n_e + n_τ = 13 + 23 = 36 = **6² = N_f²**  
The sum of the lightest and heaviest charged lepton mode indices equals the square of the flavour count N_f = χ(CP⁵) = 6.

**Outer neutrino sum:**  
n_ν₁ + n_ν₃ = 10 + 22 = **32 = 2⁵**  
The first and third neutrino mode indices sum to a power of two. The middle neutrino (15) does not participate: 32 − 15 = 17 = p (the g₂₂ Dirac eigenstate count).

### §11. Modular structure (mod n_s)

Writing n = n_s · a + r, the working set partitions into four residue classes mod n_s = 4:

| r | Members | Character |
|---|---------|----------|
| 0 | 0 (γ), 4 (s), 16 (k₀), 20 (c), 72 (t), 76 (W) | bosons and quark scale anchors; all divisible by n_s |
| 1 | 1 (d), 13 (e), 17 (p), 81 (Z) | d=3 ground state, d=6 ground state, g₂₂ parameter, Z boson |
| 2 | 10 (ν₁), 22 (ν₃) | **exclusively ν₁ and ν₃** |
| 3 | 3 (u), 15 (ν₂), 23 (τ), 35 (μ), 95 (H) | up quark plus lepton/Higgs cluster |

Residue 2 contains only ν₁ and ν₃. The algebraic reason: n_ν₃ − n_ν₁ = n_ν₂ − n_up = 15 − 3 = 12 = 3·n_s ≡ 0 mod n_s. The second neutrino escapes to residue 3 because S(n_up,4) − S(n_up,3) = q = 5 ≡ 1 mod 4, not 0.

### §12. Factor 19

19 is not a mode index but appears repeatedly in the high-mass boson sector.

**Identity of 19:** 19 = n_charm − 1 = S(n_s,3) − 1 = n_τ − n_s

**Occurrences:**
- n_W = n_s · 19 = 4 · 19 = 76
- n_H = (n_s+1) · 19 = 5 · 19 = 95
- n_H − n_W = 19 (W-to-Higgs gap)
- n_τ + n_ν₂ = 23 + 15 = 38 = 2 · 19

The W and Higgs are consecutive multiples of 19 with factors n_s and n_s+1. The Z does not share this factor (81 = 3⁴). The pattern establishes a W–H relationship that bypasses Z; the Z's role is instead to carry the gap q = n_Z − n_W = 5 (see §13 below).

No geometric interpretation of 19 has been found. It lives one step below the charm mode and one step above the tau's displacement from the seed.

### §13. W-Z mode gap equals the missing sector's Euler characteristic

n_Z − n_W = 5 = n_s + 1 = **χ(CP⁴)**

CP⁴ is the missing sector (d=8), excluded by the coupling fixed-point gap. The W-to-Z mode spacing equals the Euler characteristic of the one CP sector that does not appear in IDWT.

The same 5 appears in three other contexts:
- q = S(n_up,4) − S(n_up,3) = 5 (the hockey-stick increment entering g₂₂)
- n_ν₂ − n_ν₁ = 15 − 10 = 5 (the neutrino mode spacing)
- n_Z − n_W = 5 (the W-Z mode gap)

All three are the same q. Whether the χ(CP⁴) identification is structural or coincidental has not been resolved.

### §14. n_Z = N_c⁴ (N_c = 3 specific)

n_Z = 81 = 3⁴ = N_c⁴. This holds because:

n_Z = n_top + N_c² = N_c(N_c+1)(N_c+3) + N_c²

Setting this equal to N_c⁴ requires N_c⁴ − N_c(N_c+1)(N_c+3) − N_c² = 0, which factors as N_c(N_c−3)(N_c+1)² = 0. This is satisfied only at N_c = 3 (and the unphysical N_c = 0, −1).

**The form that generalises:** n_Z = n_top + N_c² = n_top + n_up². Valid for any N_c. The form n_Z = N_c⁴ is a numerical coincidence of our world's N_c = 3.

### §15. Dead ends

**Pion mass from sector scale arithmetic**  
Conjecture: m_π ≈ some geometric or arithmetic combination of sector scales or mode indices.  
Checked:  
- √(f_π · Λ_QCD) = √(94.04 · 282) ≈ 163 MeV — not pion mass  
- √(m_scale₃ · S(n_s,3)) = √(4.702 · 94.04) ≈ 21 MeV — not pion mass  
- m_scale₂ · S(n_down, 2) = 27.47 · 1 = 27.5 MeV — not pion mass  
Result: no clean sector-scale formula reproduces 135–140 MeV. Pion is a composite; its mass involves chiral symmetry breaking not captured by bare index arithmetic.

**"24 = dimension of CP² × CP³ isometry"**  
Conjecture: 19 + q = 19 + 5 = 24 equals the dimension of the product isometry SU(3)×SU(4).  
Checked: dim SU(3) = 8, dim SU(4) = 15, sum = 23. U(3)×U(4) gives 25. No standard reading gives 24.  
Result: claim is incorrect. 24 does not appear to have a natural role here.

**All three neutrino mode indices sharing residue 2 mod n_s**  
Conjecture: n_ν₁, n_ν₂, n_ν₃ all ≡ 2 mod 4.  
Checked: n_ν₁ = 10 ≡ 2 ✓, n_ν₃ = 22 ≡ 2 ✓, n_ν₂ = 15 ≡ 3 ✗.  
Result: ν₂ is in residue 3 because q = S(n_up,4)−S(n_up,3) ≡ 1 mod 4. The correct statement is that residue 2 is exclusively {ν₁, ν₃} — see §11.

**n_Z = N_c⁴ as a structural theorem**  
Conjecture: n_Z = N_c⁴ is a general algebraic identity independent of the specific value N_c = 3.  
Checked: requires (N_c−3)(N_c+1)² = 0, satisfied only at N_c = 3.  
Result: n_Z = N_c⁴ is a numerical coincidence. Document as n_Z = n_top + N_c² which holds generally, with the N_c = 3 special case noted. See §14.

**d=2 modes at n < 76 from Vandermonde combinations**  
Conjecture: some fermion (n,d) pair produces a Vandermonde value g = n+d−1 that could serve as a lower d=2 boson mode.  
Checked: all fermion Vandermonde values are {3, 6, 6, 14, 18, 19, 23, 26, 32, 40, 75}. All are occupied as mode indices in their own sectors; none is available as a d=2 boson. The gap n=1..75 in d=2 is the fermion mass range as seen from d=2's slower-growing simplex numbers.  
Result: no d=2 modes exist in the gap for algebraic reasons. See §2.

**Sum of occupied sector dimensions having structural meaning**  
Checked: 2+3+4+5+6+10 = 30 = (n_s+1)·N_f = 5·6. Noted but no derivation connects this product to the sector set selection rule.  
Result: appears coincidental. Filed for later.

**n_ν₁ + n_ν₃ = 2⁵ having sector interpretation**  
Checked: 10 + 22 = 32 = 2⁵. No Clifford periodicity, spinor dimension, or sector-geometry interpretation was found for 32 in this context.  
Result: verified identity, no mechanism found yet. Kept in §10.
