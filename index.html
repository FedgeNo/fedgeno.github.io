The Core Idea

IDWT rests on five postulates.

  1. Reality is a single complex wave Ψ∞ on an infinite-dimensional manifold M_∞.
  2. Our observable 3D universe is a fixed slice through this space: ψ_obs = Ψ∞(r, ξ⁰, t).
  3. The hidden dimensions are not compactified — they are macroscopic (≳ 6 mm, constrained by hydrogen spectroscopy).
  4. Mass is the cumulative count of hidden microstates accessible at a given excitation level.
  5. Gravity emerges from the back-reaction of |Ψ∞|² on the 4D projected geometry.

The mass formula is combinatorial, not fitted. Mode n in sector d has mass proportional to the binomial coefficient S(n,d) — the dimension of the space of degree-n polynomials in d variables:

m = m_scale(d) × S(n, d)   where   S(n,d) = C(n+d−1, d)

The sector dimensions d ∈ {2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10} are not chosen — they are the unique sequence generated by the Hopf fibration chain over the normed division algebras ℝ, ℂ, ℍ, 𝕆, terminating at d = 10 by Hurwitz's theorem.

Why It Works

  • a
    Sector dimensions are forced, not chosen. The Hopf fibration chain over ℝ, ℂ, ℍ, 𝕆 uniquely produces d = 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10. Each d corresponds to a particle family. There are no free choices in the sector assignment.
  • b
    The generation law is a combinatorial theorem. The hockey-stick identity S(n,d) = S(n,d−1) + S(n−1,d) forces n(μ) = n(charm) + n(ν₂) = 20 + 15 = 35, and every other generation relation. The lepton spectrum is not fitted — it is the unique fixed point of the combinatorial map applied to seeds {1, 4}.
  • c
    Two measured constants, the full spectrum. With m_e and m_W as the two empirical inputs from measurement, the theory derives all quark and lepton masses, W, Z, Higgs, ρ meson, sin²θ_W, neutrino mass ratios, proton and neutron magnetic moments, and the axial coupling g_A.
